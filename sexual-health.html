Understanding the Therapy

What is
Testosterone
Replacement Therapy?

"TRT is a medically supervised hormone therapy that restores testosterone levels to an optimal physiological range — addressing the root cause of low-T symptoms, not just masking them."

Testosterone is the primary male sex hormone, governing energy, muscle mass, libido, mood, bone density, and metabolic health.
Natural decline begins around age 30, with levels dropping 1–2% per year. By 40, many men experience clinically significant deficiency.
TRT delivers exogenous testosterone via injectable, topical, or subcutaneous methods to restore levels to the optimal 500–900 ng/dL range.
Ongoing monitoring of testosterone, estradiol, hematocrit, and PSA ensures safety and protocol optimization throughout treatment.
Recognizing Low Testosterone

Signs You May
Be a Candidate

Low testosterone affects 1 in 4 men over 30. These are the most common clinical presentations:

⚡
Chronic Fatigue
Persistent low energy, poor motivation, and unexplained exhaustion regardless of sleep quality.
🧠
Brain Fog
Difficulty concentrating, reduced mental clarity, memory lapses, and cognitive slowness.
💪
Muscle Loss
Declining strength, muscle atrophy despite training, and increasing body fat — especially abdominal.
🔥
Low Libido
Diminished sex drive, reduced sexual performance, and decreased interest in intimacy.
😔
Mood Changes
Irritability, mild depression, increased anxiety, and reduced sense of well-being.
🦴
Bone Density
Reduced bone mineral density, increased fracture risk, and joint discomfort over time.
